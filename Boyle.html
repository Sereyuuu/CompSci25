BACKGROUND

Boyle's Law is a gas law that states that a gas's pressure and volume are inversely proportional. WORD PROBLEM #1

1.) The pressurized air in a scuba diver's tank is 19.0°C in temperature. With a volume of 2.53 liters, the air inside the tank is originally at a pressure of 2.00 atm. The pressure around the tank drops to 1.65 atm when the diver descends to a shallower depth. What is going to be the tank's new air volume?

GIVEN & REQUIRED

P₁ = 2 atm

P₂ = 1.65 atm

V₁ = 2.53L

V₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

SOLUTION & ANSWER

Divide both sides to 1.65atm in order to isolate V₂. In which it cancels out the symbol for the atmosphere (atm), which makes us now multiply 2 to 2.53L. Multiply them both and divide them by 1.65 which will make the final volume to 3.07L.

The new air volume of the tank is now 3.07L.

SIGNIFICANCE

Sometimes Boyles law can be important in our daily lives too like in this scenario where you scuba dive, the pressure affects the volume of air in diving tanks which is really important to allow to keep the scuba divers safe. Boyle's main goal is to demonstrate how the pressure and the volume of the gas have an inverse relationship. Another example is medical syringes since when you push the syringe down, the volume inside the syringe decreases while pressure increases. In which it forces the medicine/liquid inside the syringe to get out.

WORD PROBLEM #2

2.) A nurse is preparing a syringe for a vaccination. The syringe intially contains 10mL at standard pressure. The nurse pulled the syringe back which results the volume being 25mL. What is the new pressure of the air inside of the syringe?

GIVEN & REQUIRED

P₁ = 1atm

P₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

V₁ = 10mL

V₂ = 25mL

Why is P₁=1atm?

The problem specified that the syringe initially contains 10mL at standard pressure, in which we have to find the value of the standard pressure which is always 1atm.

SOLUTION & ANSWER

Divide both sides to 25mL in order to isolate P₂. In which it cancels out the symbol for the millimeters (mL), which makes us now multiply 1atm to 10. Multiply them both and divide them by 25 which will make the final volume to 0.4atm.

The new pressure of the air inside the syringe is 0.4atm.
