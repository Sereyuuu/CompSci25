DEFINITION

Gay Lussac’s law came from the idea of a french chemist known as Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac (1778-1850), who proposed the fundamental gas law known as gay-lussac’s law in 1808 which states that the pressure of gases held at constant volume is directly proportional to the temperature of Kelvin. So if temperature increases, pressure also increases and vice versa. To support this, if a gas is heated, its molecules gain more energy, which results in them moving faster. If the gas particles were within a container, there would be more impacts on its walls and would cause an increase in pressure due the collisions between gas particles around the container walls from the increase in the gas particles’ mobility from the increase in temperature.

Transforming Celsius to Kelvin

This rule is no different from Charles' Theory. One again, transforming Celsius to Kelvin is very important before you start solving using Gay-Lussac's Law. This is to avoid any temperature that are in negative values hence why we don't use neither Celsius and Fahrenheit

To transform Celsius to Kelvin, we just need to add 273 to any number of Celsius. For an example, let's transform 45C° into Kelvin. 45 added by 273K is equal to 318K.

WORD PROBLEM #1

1.) Nitrogen Oxide gas was heated from 19°C to 80°C. Whilst the volume is kept constant, the initial pressure was 2,000 Pa. What would be the pressure after the temperature of the gas changed?

Transforming Celsius to Kelvin

Add 273 to the value of Celsius

GIVEN & REQUIRED

P₁ = 2000 Pa

P₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

T₁ = 292K

T₂ = 353K

SOLUTION & ANSWER

Cross multiply the given values after placing the formula. Divide both sides by 292K to isolate the P₂. Then multiply 2000Pa to 353, divide it 292 afterwards. In which makes the final pressure 2,417.81Pa

If the temperature of the gas changed, the new pressure would be 2,417.81Pa

2.) A gas tank of methane heated to 70°C was compressed with pressure of 2500 kPa. What would be the temperature of the gas tank if it were compressed to 4,000 kPa?

Transforming Celsius to Kelvin

Again, add 273 to the value of Celsius

GIVEN & REQUIRED

P₁ = 2,500kPa

P₂ = 4,000kPa

T₁ = 343K

T₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

SOLUTION & ANSWER

Cross multiply the given values after placing the formula. Divide both sides by 2500kPa to isolate the T₂. Multiply 4000 to 343K, then divide it to 2500. Which makes the final temperature 548.8K

Since celsius is needed for the problem due to the usage of celsius in the problem, we will convert the kelvin temperature to celsius by subtracting 273 from the kelvin temperature

The temperature of the gas tank would be 275.8 C° if it were compressed to 4,000 kPa.
