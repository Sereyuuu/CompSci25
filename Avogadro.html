DEFINITION

Avogadro's Law states that equal volumes of all gases under identical temperature and pressure circumstances contain an equal number of molecules. In where volume and the moles have a directly propotional when pressure is kept constant. Meaning that when volume is increased, the number of moles also increase.

FORMULA

NOTE: (After placing the given data, solve the problem using cross multiplication)

V₁ = Initial Volume, V₂ = Final Volume,

n₁ = Initial Mole, and n₂ = Final Mole


TRANSFORMING GRAMS TO MOLES

To find the molar mass, make sure to take note of the elements. Then find the atomic mass for each of the element. You can find the atomic mass below the element in the periodic table. Then add it all together resulting that would be your molar mass


EXAMPLE:

You are conducting an experiment in your chemistry class and need to calculate the number of moles of water (H₂O) in a sample. The sample weighs exactly 36 grams.


Knowing the formula, calculate the molar mass for H₂O first by finding the element's atomic mass. In which H₂O has 2 hydrogen atoms (1.008 g/mol x 2 = 2.016) and 1 oxygen atom (16 g/mol x 1 = 16). Add them together making the molar mass to 18.016 g/mol.

Then divide 36 grams to 18.016 g/mol which will result to 2 moles. Therefore the number of moles of water (H₂O) that weighs 36 grams is 2 moles.

WORD PROBLEM #1

1.) During Grade 10 Endurance's laboratory activity, someone inflated a balloon, which had a volume of 5.30 L when filled with 0.435 moles of air. However, while playing with the balloon, the air was deflated, resulting in a new volume of 3.30 L. How many moles of gas escaped the balloon?

GIVEN & REQUIRED

V₁ = 5.30L

V₂ = 3.30L

n₁ = 0.435 mol

n₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

SOLUTION & ANSWER

Insert what was given into the formula, then cross-multiply the given values. Divide both sides with 5.30L in order to isolate n₂. Then multiply 3.30 with 0.435mol, after that divide it by 5.30. Which will make the answer 0.164

There are 0.164 mol of moles of gas that escaped the balloon.

SIGNIFICANCE

Avogadro’s Law is important in our lives as it can used to determine the amount of gas present within an object and the volume of gas present. A good example of Avogadro’s law in action is by breathing, the more air we breathe in, the volume of our lungs increases, and vice versa. Another good example is the deflation of air within the tires of cars, which can be crucial to a person’s safety.

WORD PROBLEM #2

2.) A ccontainer contains 3.00 g of oxygen (O₂) at room temperature. More oxygen is added to the container, and the volume is adjusted so that the gas pressure remains the same. How many grams of oxygen were added to the container if the volume was changed from 3.00 L to 3.60 L?

Make sure before solving, convert the grams into moles for it to be our intial mole. 2 oxygen atoms are present in 1 molecule hence it's O₂. The atomic mass of Oxygen is 16 so 16 multiplied by 2 is 32 which makes that our molar mass. Divide 3g to 32g/mol which will make our intial mole to 0.09 mol

GIVEN & REQUIRED

V₁ = 3L

V₂ = 3.60L

n₁ = 0.09 mol

n₂ = ? (REQUIRED)

SOLUTION & ANSWER

0.108mol is not our final answer as we need to know how many grams of oxygen were added in the container. To do this we need to convert the moles to grams by multiplying the moles to the molar mass.

Multiply the final mole (0.108 mol) to the molar mass of O₂ (32g/mol) where the product is 3.46g. Subtract the initial mass (3g) to the final mass (3.46g) making our final answer to be 0.46g

0.46g were added in the container if the volume changed from 3L to 3.60L.
